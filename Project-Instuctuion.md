📌 Prototype Development Guidelines

To ensure fair evaluation and a smooth demonstration experience, all teams are requested to follow the guidelines below:

• Focus on building a working prototype rather than a large number of incomplete features.

• Implement only those features that can be demonstrated successfully during evaluation.

• Avoid adding static buttons, pages, or modules that do not perform any actual functionality.

• The core solution/main feature addressing the chosen problem statement must be fully functional and demonstrable.

• Ensure that the prototype runs without major errors, crashes, or broken workflows.

• Quality is preferred over quantity. A few well-implemented features are better than many unfinished ones.
Even one main functionality is enough.

• Any AI, ML, cybersecurity, or advanced technology claims made in the presentation should be supported by a working implementation or a clearly demonstrated prototype.

• Teams should be able to explain the complete workflow of their solution from input to output.

• Test the application thoroughly before the mock and final presentations.

• The prototype should clearly showcase how the proposed solution solves the selected problem statement.

• Teams are encouraged to maintain a clean and user-friendly interface, but functionality will be prioritized over design.

⚠️ Evaluation will primarily focus on the effectiveness, functionality, innovation, and feasibility of the working solution rather than the number of features implemented.
